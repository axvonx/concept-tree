# Digital Electronics

Digital electronics represents information as discrete binary states — high (1) or low (0). By encoding everything as bits, digital systems gain enormous resilience to noise: as long as a signal is distinguishable as "high" or "low," its exact voltage doesn't matter.

From simple NAND gates to multi-billion-transistor processors, digital electronics scales through abstraction layers: gates → flip-flops → registers → ALUs → CPUs. This layering is what makes modern computing possible.

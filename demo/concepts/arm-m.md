# ARM Cortex-M

The ARM Cortex-M is a family of 32-bit RISC processor cores licensed to dozens of silicon vendors (ST, NXP, Nordic, Microchip, and more). It's the dominant architecture for embedded systems: Cortex-M0/M0+ are the smallest and lowest-power; Cortex-M4/M7 add hardware floating point and DSP instructions; Cortex-M33 adds TrustZone security.

Every major toolchain supports Cortex-M: GCC, LLVM/clang, IAR, and Keil. The common CMSIS headers provide a hardware abstraction layer, and frameworks like STM32 HAL, Zephyr, and FreeRTOS run on top. Cortex-M devices are the backbone of industrial, medical, and automotive electronics.

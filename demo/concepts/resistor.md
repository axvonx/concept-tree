# Resistor

A resistor limits current flow in proportion to its resistance value (Ohm's Law: V = IR). It's the most basic component in electronics — used for setting bias points, voltage division, pull-ups, current limiting, and signal attenuation.

Resistors are specified by value (Ω, kΩ, MΩ), tolerance (1% or 5% common), power rating (¼W for signal-level work), and temperature coefficient. Carbon film are cheap; metal film are more precise and stable.

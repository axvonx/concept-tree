# Operational Amplifiers

An op-amp (operational amplifier) is a high-gain differential amplifier. With negative feedback it becomes one of the most versatile analog building blocks: voltage follower, inverting amplifier, summing amplifier, integrator, differentiator, comparator, active filter.

The ideal op-amp has infinite gain, infinite input impedance, zero output impedance, and infinite bandwidth. Real op-amps trade off these properties — choosing the right one means matching bandwidth, noise, supply voltage, and input range to your application.

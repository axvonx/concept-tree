# Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

The uncertainty principle states that there is a fundamental limit to the precision with which certain pairs of physical properties, such as position and momentum, can be simultaneously known.

$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$

This is not a statement about the limitations of measurement technology, but rather about the nature of the quantum system itself.

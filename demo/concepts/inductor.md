# Inductor

An inductor stores energy in a magnetic field around a coil of wire. It resists changes in current — passing DC easily but impeding AC. Combined with capacitors, inductors form LC filters and resonant tanks used in radio, switching power supplies, and RF circuits.

Inductors are specified by inductance (henries), DC resistance (DCR), saturation current, and self-resonant frequency (SRF). For power applications choose an inductor rated well above your peak current to avoid saturation.

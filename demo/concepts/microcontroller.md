# Microcontrollers

A microcontroller (MCU) is a complete computer on a single chip: CPU, RAM, flash memory, and peripherals (UART, SPI, I²C, ADC, PWM) all integrated together. Unlike general-purpose processors, MCUs are designed to interface directly with hardware — reading sensors, driving motors, and responding to the physical world in real time.

MCUs are categorized by their CPU architecture. AVR is the classic 8-bit workhorse; ARM Cortex-M is the dominant 32-bit family; the RP2040 is a newer dual-core design. Choosing an MCU means balancing compute power, peripheral set, power consumption, and ecosystem.

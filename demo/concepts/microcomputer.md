# Microcomputers

Microcomputers are complete computer systems small enough to fit on a single board, typically in the palm of your hand. They run full operating systems (usually Linux), support networking, and expose hardware GPIO for interfacing with sensors, motors, and displays.

They sit in the gap between microcontrollers (bare-metal, real-time, milliwatts) and desktop machines (full OS, high compute, high power). For robotics, home automation, and networked edge devices, a microcomputer is often the right tool.

# Bluetooth-Capable Microcontrollers

Bluetooth-capable MCUs integrate a 2.4GHz radio alongside the processor core, enabling wireless communication without an external module. The two dominant standards are **Bluetooth Classic** (for audio and legacy devices) and **Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)**, optimized for battery-powered IoT devices that send small bursts of data.

BLE is pervasive: fitness trackers, smart home sensors, wireless keyboards, medical devices, and beacons all use it. Choosing a Bluetooth MCU means evaluating RF performance, stack certification, power in deep sleep, and antenna options (PCB trace vs. external).

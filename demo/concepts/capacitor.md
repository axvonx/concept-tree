# Capacitor

A capacitor stores energy in an electric field between two conductive plates. In circuits it blocks DC while passing AC — making it essential for filtering, decoupling, and timing. Capacitors are rated by capacitance (farads), voltage rating, and dielectric type.

**Ceramic** capacitors (10pF–10µF) are small, cheap, and good for decoupling. **Electrolytic** capacitors (1µF–10,000µF) offer high capacitance but are polarized and must be installed the right way. **Film** capacitors offer the best stability for precision applications.

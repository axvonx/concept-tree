# Chaos Theory

Chaos theory is the study of dynamical systems that are highly sensitive to initial conditions — a phenomenon popularly known as the butterfly effect. Small differences in initial conditions yield widely diverging outcomes, making long-term prediction practically impossible.

## Key Concepts

- **Lorenz attractor** — a set of chaotic solutions to the Lorenz system, forming a butterfly-shaped strange attractor
- **Sensitive dependence** — tiny perturbations grow exponentially over time
- **Strange attractors** — fractal structures that chaotic trajectories orbit without ever repeating
- **Bifurcation** — how small changes in a parameter cause sudden qualitative changes in system behavior

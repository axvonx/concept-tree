# Electronics

Electronics is the science and engineering of controlling electric charge to process information, convert energy, and drive machines. Every modern device — from a wristwatch to a supercomputer — is built from the same fundamental building blocks: components that resist, store, amplify, and switch electrical signals.

The field divides broadly into **analog** electronics, where signals are continuous voltages and currents, and **digital** electronics, where everything is reduced to 1s and 0s. In practice, most real systems bridge both worlds — sensors produce analog signals, microcontrollers digitize them, and DACs convert results back to drive speakers or motors.

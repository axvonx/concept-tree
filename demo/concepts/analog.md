# Analog Electronics

Analog electronics deals with signals that vary continuously over time — voltage and current that represent real-world phenomena like sound, temperature, and light. Unlike digital systems, the signal itself *is* the information at every moment.

Key concepts include impedance matching, signal-to-noise ratio, bandwidth, and gain. Analog design requires thinking about how components interact across frequency ranges, not just at a single operating point. A well-designed analog front-end is what lets a digital system interact faithfully with the physical world.
